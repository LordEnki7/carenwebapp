Right to Remain Silent
CRITICAL
You have the absolute right to remain silent. Clearly state "I am invoking my right to remain silent" — you do not need to answer questions beyond identifying yourself (name, ID in TX).
5th Amendment · Texas Transportation Code §521.049

Right to Record Police
KNOW THIS
Texas law permits recording police officers performing their duties in public. Do not interfere with their actions. You may record openly and do not need permission.
1st Amendment · TX Penal Code §38.15

Vehicle Search Rights
ADVISORY
Police need probable cause or your consent to search your vehicle. You may calmly say "I do not consent to a search." Refusing consent cannot be used against you.
4th Amendment · Texas v. Brown

Right to an Attorney
CRITICAL
If detained or arrested, clearly state "I want an attorney." All questioning must stop immediately. Do not answer any questions until your attorney is present.
6th Amendment · Miranda v. Arizona
